## PRESENTATION DELIVERY TIPS

### Before the Meeting:
1. **Practice the pitch** (you + Said together, at least 2x)
2. **Divide slides** (who presents what - alternate, don't let one person dominate)
3. **Prepare for objections** (see next document: Meeting Script with objection handling)
4. **Test tech** (screen share, slides load properly, backup plan if tech fails)
5. **Print leave-behind** (proposal PDF, 1-2 pages summary)

### During the Meeting:
1. **Start with rapport** (5 min small talk, build comfort)
2. **Ask permission** ("Mind if we share our findings? This will take about 30-40 minutes...")
3. **Pause frequently** (check in: "Does this resonate?" "Questions so far?")
4. **Read the room** (if they're glazing over, skip slides or speed up)
5. **Focus on them** (not you - use "you" more than "we")

### After Slide 12 (Success Metrics):
**STOP AND CHECK IN:**
- "So far, does this make sense?"
- "Is this addressing the challenge you're facing?"
- "Before we talk about how we'd work together, what questions do you have?"

**Why:** This is the halfway point. If they're not bought in yet, don't plow ahead into pricing. Address concerns first.

### Closing the Meeting:
1. **Don't ask "What do you think?"** (too vague)
2. **Ask for the sale:** "Based on what we've shown you, do you want to move forward?"
3. **If yes:** "Great, let's talk timeline. Can you start Week of Feb 24?"
4. **If hesitation:** "What concerns do you have?" (address objections)
5. **If no:** "What would need to change for this to be a yes?"

### After the Meeting:
1. **Send proposal within 2 hours** (strike while hot)
2. **Follow-up email** (recap key points, next steps)
3. **Set deadline** ("We need to know by Monday to start next week")
4. **Stay in touch** (text/call Fri if no response)

## SLIDE DESIGN GUIDELINES

**Visual Style:**
- **Clean, professional** (no clipart, no Comic Sans)
- **Lots of white space** (don't cram slides)
- **Big fonts** (minimum 24pt for body text)
- **High-quality images** (no pixelated graphics)
- **Consistent branding** (same colors, fonts throughout)

**Color Palette Suggestion:**
- **Primary:** Navy blue or charcoal (professional)
- **Accent:** Teal or silver (Rhinestone's brand colors)
- **Highlights:** Orange or green (call-to-action, urgency)
- **Backgrounds:** White or light gray (easy to read)

**Fonts:**
- **Headlines:** Montserrat, Raleway, or Poppins (bold, modern)
- **Body:** Open Sans, Lato, or Helvetica (clean, readable)
- **Avoid:** Anything too decorative or hard to read

**Icons & Graphics:**
- Use icons from: The Noun Project, Flaticon, or built-in PowerPoint icons
- Consistent style (all outline or all filled, not mixed)
- Strategic use (enhance understanding, not decoration)
