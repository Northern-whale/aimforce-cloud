**Key Message:** You're selling employer branding without an employer brand.

**Say this:**

> 
> - No company website
> - No LinkedIn company page
> - No social media presence
> - Basically zero digital footprint
> 
> Now, that's not criticism - it's an opportunity. But here's the challenge: You're selling employer branding services to companies, and the first thing prospects do is Google you. When they find nothing, that creates a credibility gap.

> Every sales conversation has a built-in objection: 'If you're so good at employer branding, why don't YOU have a brand?'"

**Pause. Let that land.**

**Then:**
> "We estimate this is costing you $100K-$250K per year in lost opportunities. Prospects who would hire you, but ghost after Googling. Deals you can't close because you can't show proof. And you're forced to compete on price instead of value."

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### "We need to think about it"
**Response:**
> "Absolutely, this is a big decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Budget? Timeline? Proof of concept? Let's address those concerns now so you have what you need to decide."

---

### "Can you guarantee results?"
**Response:**
> "I can guarantee execution: 36 posts published, weekly reporting, all deliverables. What I can't guarantee is that you'll close X deals - there are too many variables I don't control.
>
> But here's what I know: Industry data shows that consistent LinkedIn activity + optimized profile + thought leadership content generates 5-10 inquiries per 100 followers gained. If you gain 500-1,000 followers, math says you'll get 25-100 inquiries. Converting even 2% of those to deals gets you profitable.
>
> Plus we have the 30-day guarantee - if you don't see progress, you get your money back."

---

### "That's expensive"
**Response:**
> "Compared to what? If you hired a full-time marketer, that's $4K-$6K/month plus benefits. If you hired a traditional agency, that's $5K-$10K/month. We're $2,997/month and you get AI-powered systems that work 24/7.
>
> But more importantly - if this generates even one client, it pays for itself 4x over. It's not an expense, it's an investment with a clear ROI."

---

### "We don't have budget right now"
**Response:**
> "I get that. When would budget open up? Is this a quarterly thing? Annual?
>
> Also - we have the split payment option. $4,496 now, $4,495 in 45 days. Does that help?
>
> Or here's another thought: If you close one deal in the next 30 days from your current efforts, use that revenue to fund this. Then we're building on momentum."

---

### "Can we see examples / case studies?"
**Response:**
> "Honest answer: You're our first employer branding client. We've built AI systems for other industries, but recruiting is new for us.
>
> That's actually why we're offering competitive pricing and documenting this as a case study. You're getting a discount because you're helping us prove the model.
>
> If you want an agency with 50 case studies, we're not it. But if you want cutting-edge AI, fair pricing, and a partner who's as invested in your success as you are - that's us.
>
> Think of it this way: You're getting the same quality you'd get from a $10K/month agency, at one-third the price, because you're the pilot."

---

### "What if it doesn't work?"
**Response:**
> "Define 'doesn't work.' If you mean we don't publish posts or build the website - that won't happen, we control that. If you mean you don't get followers - very unlikely with consistent content. If you mean you don't close deals - that's partly on the quality of your sales process, which we can't control.
>
> But here's the safety net: 30-day guarantee. After Month 1, if you're not seeing progress (follower growth, engagement, profile views trending up), we refund your money.
>
> And realistically - worst case, you still get a website, optimized LinkedIn, 36 pieces of content, and a documented case study. Even if you never close a client from it (unlikely), those assets have value."

---

### "We need to talk to our partner/board/advisor"
**Response:**
> "Makes sense. Who needs to be involved in this decision? Can we schedule a follow-up call with them? Or can we send them the proposal to review?
>
> What's the timeline for that conversation? We need to start by Feb 26 to hit the 90-day timeline, which means decision by Monday."

---

### "Can we do a 1-month trial?"
**Response:**
> "I understand the desire to test, but here's the challenge: Month 1 is foundation (website, LinkedIn setup, strategy). Month 2 is execution (content starts publishing, growth begins). Month 3 is results (momentum builds, leads come in).
>
> If we stop at Month 1, you won't see ROI - you'll just have a website and 4 posts. That's not enough to prove anything.
>
> What we CAN do is the 30-day guarantee: If after Month 1 you're not happy with the progress, we refund that $2,997. But you need to commit to 3 months so we have time to deliver results."
