# Production Workflow Guide
## From Filming to Publishing - Complete Step-by-Step Process

---

## Overview

This guide covers the complete production workflow for Tulsi's Pet Store Adventures, designed so that non-experts can create professional-quality YouTube content.

**Time Investment per Episode:** 6-10 hours total
- Filming: 1-2 hours
- Scripting (AI-assisted): 30-60 minutes
- Voice generation: 15-30 minutes
- Editing: 3-5 hours
- Publishing/promotion: 30-60 minutes

## PHASE 2: FILMING

### Step 2.1: Setting Up Shots

**Basic Composition Rules:**
- **Rule of Thirds:** Place Tulsi slightly off-center
- **Eye Level:** Camera at Tulsi's eye level for most shots
- **Headroom:** Small space above Tulsi's head, not too much
- **Clean Background:** Avoid clutter, use store displays strategically

**Lighting Tips:**
- Natural light from windows is best (diffused, not direct sunlight)
- Avoid overhead fluorescent lights (creates harsh shadows)
- Light should be in front of subject, not behind (no silhouettes)
- Consistent lighting throughout shoot (same time of day)

### Step 2.2: Shot Types to Capture

**Essential Shots (Get these every time):**

1. **A-Roll (Tulsi "talking" shots):**
   - 3-5 different locations around store
   - 10-15 seconds each
   - Tulsi looking at camera, natural expressions
   - Multiple takes (you'll choose best in editing)

2. **B-Roll (Supporting footage):**
   - Wide shots: Entire store, different aisles
   - Medium shots: Customers browsing, employees working
   - Close-ups: Products, treats, toys, Tulsi's reactions
   - Action shots: Tulsi playing, walking, interacting
   - Detail shots: Ingredient labels, price tags, textures

3. **Transition Shots:**
   - Doors opening/closing
   - Tulsi walking through frame
   - Products changing
   - Time passing (clock, lighting changes)

**Pro Tips:**
- **5-second rule:** Hold every shot for at least 5 seconds (easier to edit)
- **Get doubles:** Film important moments from 2 angles
- **Sound:** Get 30 seconds of "room tone" (silence with store ambiance)
- **Variety:** Mix wide, medium, and close shots

### Step 2.3: Filming Checklist

Before you stop filming, verify you have:
- [ ] At least 5 Tulsi "talking" positions
- [ ] 20+ B-roll clips (various subjects)
- [ ] Any special moments (customer interactions, product tests)
- [ ] Clean audio (check for background noise)
- [ ] Enough lighting consistency
- [ ] Release forms signed (if featuring customers/pets)

### Step 3.1: Review Footage

Watch all footage and note:
- Best Tulsi expressions/moments

- [ ] Timing feels right (read aloud while watching footage)

**Read Aloud Test:**
- Time yourself reading the script

- Does it sound natural?

## PHASE 4: VOICE GENERATION

### Step 4.1: ElevenLabs Setup

**Account Setup:**
1. Create account at elevenlabs.io
2. Choose plan: Starter ($5/month) or Creator ($22/month)
3. Create custom voice for Tulsi

**Creating Tulsi's Voice:**

**Method 1: Voice Design (Recommended)**
1. Go to Voice Library ‚Üí Voice Design
2. Set parameters:
   - Gender: Female
   - Age: Young Adult
   - Accent: American - General
   - Tone: Warm, Friendly
3. Generate samples until you find the perfect voice
4. Save as "Tulsi"

**Method 2: Instant Voice Cloning (If you have voice actor)**
1. Record 1-2 minutes of sample audio
2. Upload to ElevenLabs
3. Clone voice
4. Save as "Tulsi"

### Step 4.2: Generate Voiceover

**Settings for Best Quality:**
- Voice: Tulsi (your custom voice)
- Model: Eleven Multilingual v2 (best quality)
- Voice Settings:
  - Stability: 70%
  - Clarity + Similarity Enhancement: 80%
  - Style Exaggeration: 35%
  - Speaker Boost: ON

**Generation Process:**
1. Paste script in sections (not all at once - break into scenes)
2. Generate each section
3. Listen for quality
4. Re-generate if needed (try adjusting settings)
5. Download as MP3 (highest quality)

**Pro Tips:**
- Add SSML tags for emphasis: `<prosody rate="slow">slower speech</prosody>`
- Use commas and periods for natural pausing
- Break up long paragraphs
- Generate multiple takes of emotional moments

### Step 4.3: Quality Check

Listen to full voiceover and verify:
- [ ] Clear pronunciation
- [ ] Natural pacing
- [ ] Appropriate emotion/energy
- [ ] Consistent volume
- [ ] No artifacts or glitches
- [ ] Sounds like Tulsi character

If issues found: Regenerate problem sections

## PHASE 5: VIDEO EDITING

### Step 5.1: Software Setup

**Recommended Free Options:**
- **CapCut** (easiest, great for beginners)
- **DaVinci Resolve** (more powerful, steeper learning curve)
- **iMovie** (Mac users, simple)

**Paid Options:**
- Adobe Premiere Pro ($20/month - professional)
- Final Cut Pro ($300 one-time - Mac only)

**We'll use CapCut for this guide (works on desktop & mobile):**

### Step 5.2: Import & Organize

**Project Setup:**
1. Create new project: "Tulsi Episode [#]"
2. Set aspect ratio: 16:9 (YouTube standard)
3. Frame rate: 30fps or 60fps (match your footage)
4. Import files:
   - All video clips
   - Tulsi voiceover (MP3)
   - Background music
   - Intro/outro graphics
   - Sound effects

**Organize Timeline:**
- **Track 1:** Main video footage
- **Track 2:** Tulsi voiceover
- **Track 3:** Background music
- **Track 4:** Sound effects
- **Track 5:** Graphics/text overlays

### Step 5.3: Rough Cut Assembly

**Step-by-step:**

1. **Drag voiceover to Track 2 first** (this is your guide!)

2. **Add intro sequence (0:00-0:05):**
   - Animated intro template
   - "Tulsi's Pet Store Adventures" title
   - Upbeat music sting

3. **Match video to voiceover:**
   - Listen to a section of voiceover
   - Find matching B-roll that illustrates what Tulsi is saying
   - Drag video clip to Track 1, sync with voiceover
   - Trim to fit
   - Repeat for entire script

4. **Use Tulsi "talking" shots strategically:**
   - Every 20-30 seconds, cut to Tulsi looking at camera
   - Creates connection with audience
   - Varies visuals
   - Don't overuse (60% B-roll, 40% Tulsi shots)

5. **Add outro (last 10 seconds):**
   - Outro template
   - Subscribe button animation
   - Next episode tease
   - Social media handles

### Step 5.4: Fine-Tuning Edit

**Pacing:**
- Cut on action when possible
- Vary shot lengths: 2-5 seconds average
- Faster cuts for exciting moments
- Longer shots for emotional/reflective moments
- No shot shorter than 1 second (too jarring)

**Transitions:**
- Use simple cuts for 90% of transitions
- Occasional cross-dissolve for time passing
- Avoid flashy transitions (too distracting)

**Visual Interest:**
- Add slight zoom/pan to static shots (Ken Burns effect)
- Use slow motion sparingly (emotional moments)
- Crop/reframe shots if needed

### Step 5.5: Add Music & Sound

**Background Music:**
- Source: YouTube Audio Library, Epidemic Sound, Artlist
- Genre: Upbeat acoustic, light pop, instrumental
- Volume: -20dB to -25dB (quiet, not overpowering)
- Fade in/out at beginning and end
- Duck volume when Tulsi talks (automatic in most editors)

**Sound Effects:**
- Squeaky toy sounds (enhance product testing)
- Door bells (transitions)
- "Boop" or "ding" (text appearing)
- Cash register (purchases)
- Gentle whoosh (scene changes)

**Volume Levels:**
- Voiceover: -3dB to 0dB (loudest element)
- Music: -20dB to -25dB
- Sound effects: -10dB to -15dB
- Adjust for clarity - voiceover must always be clear

### Step 5.6: Graphics & Text

**Text Overlays (use sparingly):**
- Episode title card (0:05)
- Customer/pet names when introduced
- Key tips ("Remember: Check ingredients!")
- Product names/prices
- Location tags
- Social media handles (subtle, lower third)

**Design Guidelines:**
- Font: Clean, sans-serif (e.g., Montserrat, Poppins)
- Colors: Warm orange (#FF8C42), sky blue (#6BB6FF), white
- Size: Large enough to read on mobile
- Duration: On screen long enough to read 2x
- Animation: Simple slide or fade in/out

**Graphics to Add:**
- Paw print transitions
- Rating graphics (paw ratings for product reviews)
- Comparison charts
- Before/after graphics
- Episode number bug (small, corner of screen)

### Step 5.7: Color Grading

**Basic Color Correction:**
1. Adjust exposure (brightness)
2. Increase saturation slightly (+10-15%)
3. Warm up color temperature
4. Increase contrast slightly
5. Ensure Tulsi stands out visually

**CapCut Preset:** Use "Warm" or "Cozy" filter at 30-50% strength

**Goal:** Consistent, inviting, warm aesthetic

### Step 5.8: Captions/Subtitles

**Why they matter:**
- 85% of videos watched without sound
- Accessibility for hearing impaired
- Better engagement/retention
- SEO benefits

**How to add:**
1. Auto-generate in CapCut (Speech to Text)
2. Review and fix errors
3. Style: White text, black outline, bottom center
4. Font: Large, clean, easy to read
5. Also export as .SRT file for YouTube upload

### Step 5.9: Final Review Checklist

Before exporting, verify:
- [ ] Audio levels balanced (voiceover clear, music subtle)
- [ ] No visual errors (blurry footage, bad cuts)
- [ ] All text readable on mobile
- [ ] Intro/outro present
- [ ] Consistent color grading
- [ ] Captions accurate
- [ ] Runtime 5-8 minutes (ideal for YouTube algorithm)
- [ ] Strong opening hook (rewatchtest first 10 seconds)
- [ ] Clear call-to-action
- [ ] Episode number displayed

### Step 5.10: Export Settings

**YouTube Optimal Settings:**
- Format: MP4
- Resolution: 1920x1080 (1080p)
- Frame Rate: Match source (30fps or 60fps)
- Bitrate: 8-12 Mbps
- Audio: AAC, 320kbps

**File Naming:** `Tulsi_Ep[##]_[Title]_[Date].mp4`

Example: `Tulsi_Ep01_Meet_Tulsi_2025-03-01.mp4`

## PHASE 6: THUMBNAIL CREATION

### Step 6.1: Thumbnail Design Principles

**What Makes a Good Thumbnail:**
- Bright, contrasting colors
- Clear focal point (Tulsi's face)
- Readable text (3-5 words max)
- Emotionally compelling
- Looks good at small size
- Brand consistent

**Size:** 1280 x 720 pixels (16:9 ratio)

### Step 6.2: Creating Thumbnails (Canva Method)

**Steps:**
1. Go to Canva.com
2. Search template: "YouTube Thumbnail"
3. Choose blank or simple template
4. Upload best screenshot of Tulsi from video
5. Add to canvas, resize to fill left 60%
6. Add text on right 40%
7. Use brand colors (orange, blue, white)
8. Add paw print or bone graphics
9. Download as PNG

**Text Tips:**
- Font: Bold, thick (e.g., Anton, Bebas Neue)
- Outline: White text with orange outline (or vice versa)
- Shadow: Drop shadow for depth
- Size: Massive (readable at thumbnail size)

**Examples:**
- "MEET TULSI!"
- "TOP 5 TREATS!"
- "MEET COOPER!"
- "BEHIND SCENES!"
- "10 TOYS TESTED!"

### Step 6.3: Thumbnail A/B Testing

After 10-20 videos, compare:
- CTR (click-through rate) in YouTube analytics
- Which styles perform best
- Adjust template accordingly

## PHASE 9: ENGAGEMENT & ANALYTICS

### Step 9.1: First 24-Hour Push

**Critical for algorithm:**
- Reply to EVERY comment within first 24 hours
- Like all comments
- Ask follow-up questions
- Create conversation

**Response Templates:**
- "Thank you so much for watching!"
- "Great question! [Answer]"
- "So glad you enjoyed [specific moment]!"
- "What would you like to see next?"

### Step 9.2: Monitor Analytics

**Check after 48 hours:**
- Views
- Click-through rate (CTR) - aim for 4-10%
- Average view duration - aim for >50%
- Traffic sources (where are viewers coming from?)
- Audience retention graph (where do people drop off?)

**Make Adjustments:**
- Low CTR? Test new thumbnail style
- Low retention? Tighten editing, stronger hooks
- Certain topics perform better? Make more of those

### Step 9.3: Weekly Review

Every Monday, review last week's performance:
- Total views
- Subscriber growth
- Best performing video
- Worst performing video
- Comments/engagement

Document learnings in production notes for next videos.

## TROUBLESHOOTING

### Common Issues & Solutions

**Problem:** Footage is shaky
**Solution:** Use phone tripod, stabilize in editing (CapCut has stabilization)

**Problem:** Audio has echo/background noise
**Solution:** Film during quiet times, add light music to mask, use noise reduction

**Problem:** Video feels slow/boring
**Solution:** Cut 20% of content, faster cuts, stronger opening hook

**Problem:** Colors look flat
**Solution:** Increase saturation +15%, adjust contrast, use warm color grade

**Problem:** Text is hard to read
**Solution:** Larger font, thicker outline, simpler background

**Problem:** Music is too loud
**Solution:** Lower to -20dB, use auto-duck feature when voiceover plays

**Problem:** Voiceover sounds robotic
**Solution:** Regenerate with different settings, add more punctuation to script for natural pausing

**Problem:** Video too long (>10 min)
**Solution:** Cut tangents, tighten pacing, split into 2 episodes
