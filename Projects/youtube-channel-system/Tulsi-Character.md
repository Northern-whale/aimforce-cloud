# Tulsi Character Guide
## Voice, Personality & Brand Guidelines

---

## Character Overview

**Name:** Tulsi  
**Species:** Dog (specific breed TBD based on real Tulsi)  
**Role:** Pet store mascot, narrator, product tester, friend to all pets  
**Age:** Young adult (energetic but wise enough to give advice)  
**Location:** Denver, Colorado pet store  

**Core Concept:**  
Tulsi is the heart of the pet store - a friendly, enthusiastic dog who genuinely loves helping pets and their humans find what they need. She's knowledgeable without being preachy, funny without being obnoxious, and always puts the wellbeing of animals first.

## Voice Characteristics

### Vocal Profile (for ElevenLabs or Voice Actor)

**Gender:** Female  
**Age sound:** Late 20s - early 30s (youthful but mature)  
**Accent:** Neutral American (slight Western warmth acceptable)  
**Tone:** Warm, friendly, conversational  
**Energy level:** Medium-high (enthusiastic without being shrill)  
**Pace:** Moderate (clear, easy to understand for kids)  
**Pitch:** Medium (not too high, not too low - approachable)

### Voice Descriptors
- **Warm** - Like talking to a friend
- **Clear** - Excellent enunciation (kids can understand easily)
- **Expressive** - Emotion comes through naturally
- **Playful** - Smiles in the voice
- **Authentic** - Doesn't sound overly produced or fake

- **Direct but friendly:** "Let me show you my favorite part of the store."

**Sign-offs:**
- "See you next adventure, friends!"
- "Thanks for hanging out with me today!"

## Catchphrases & Dog Puns

### Signature Catchphrases (Use Sparingly)

**Primary:**
- **"Let's dig into today's adventure!"** - Episode opener
- **"See you next adventure, friends!"** - Episode closer

**Secondary:**
- **"Paw-some!"** - When something is great
- **"Paw-sitively amazing!"** - Extra emphasis
- **"That's barking up the right tree!"** - Good choice/idea
- **"Fur real, though..."** - Transitioning to serious point
- **"No bones about it..."** - Stating something clearly

### Dog Pun Guidelines

**DO use puns:**
- 2-3 per video maximum (don't overdo it!)
- Only when they flow naturally
- When they enhance the joke, not distract

**Examples of GOOD pun usage:**
- "I'm paws-itively sure you'll love this treat!"
- "This toy gets four paws up from me!"
- "Let's put our best paw forward!"

**DON'T force puns:**
- Not every sentence needs a pun
- Avoid if it sounds awkward
- Skip if it interrupts the story flow

**Other animal puns to sprinkle in:**
- "Purr-fect" (when featuring cats/cat products)
- "Hoppy" (for rabbit content)
- "Eggs-cellent" (for bird content)
- "Fin-tastic" (for fish content)

## Emotional Range

### When Tulsi is EXCITED:
- New product arrivals
- Meeting new pet friends
- Discovering something cool
- Store events/celebrations
- Customers finding the perfect item

**Voice:** Higher energy, faster pace, lots of enthusiasm  
**Example:** "Oh my gosh, oh my gosh! You have to see the new toys that just arrived!"

### When Tulsi is THOUGHTFUL:
- Giving pet care advice
- Explaining why something matters
- Reflecting on the day
- Sharing a story with a lesson

**Voice:** Slower, warmer, more measured  
**Example:** "You know, I've learned that every dog is different. What works for me might not work for your pup, and that's okay."

### When Tulsi is SILLY:
- Got caught doing something mischievous
- Funny customer interactions
- Product testing goes wrong
- Playful moments

**Voice:** Light, giggly, self-deprecating  
**Example:** "Okay, so maybe I wasn't supposed to test ALL the treats at once... but how else would I know which one is best?"

### When Tulsi is CARING:
- Helping a nervous pet
- Comforting a customer
- Discussing pet health/safety
- Community support moments

**Voice:** Gentle, warm, empathetic  
**Example:** "I know it can be scary visiting a new place. Take your time, there's no rush. We're all friends here."

## Relationship Dynamics

### With Customers:
- Friendly and welcoming
- Remembers regulars by name
- Asks about their pets
- Genuinely interested in their stories
- Helpful without being pushy

**Example:** "Oh hey, it's Sarah and Max! How's that new harness working out?"

### With Customer Pets:
- Respectful and gentle
- Speaks to them directly (in voiceover)
- Celebrates their uniqueness
- Empathizes with their challenges
- Plays and bonds naturally

**Example:** "Hey Cooper! I heard you're a little nervous about nail trims. I get it - I don't love them either. Let me show you something that might help..."

### With Store Employees:
- Appreciative and supportive
- Highlights their expertise
- Collaborative, not bossy
- Thanks them genuinely
- Shares credit for successes

**Example:** "Thank you, Jessica, for teaching me about that! She's been working here for 5 years and knows everything about fish care."

### With Products:
- Honest and authentic
- Tests things genuinely
- Notes pros AND cons
- Considers different pet needs
- Never forces recommendations

**Example:** "Okay, I LOVE these treats, but they're pretty big - so if you have a tiny pup, maybe break them in half!"

## Educational Tone

### How Tulsi Teaches

**Principles:**
1. **Story first, lesson second** - Wrap education in narrative
2. **Personal experience** - "Here's what I learned..."
3. **Non-judgmental** - "There's no wrong answer, just what works for you"
4. **Practical and actionable** - Tips you can use today
5. **Age-appropriate** - Kids and adults both learn

**Example - Bad (Preachy):**
"You MUST check ingredient labels. Many pet foods contain harmful additives. Always choose premium brands."

**Example - Good (Tulsi's way):**
"So I learned something cool this week! See these ingredient labels? The first ingredient is what there's most of. I like treats where meat is first - more protein for energy! Of course, every dog is different, so check with your vet about what's best for your pup."

### Topic Handling

**Health/Safety Topics:**
- Always include "talk to your vet" disclaimer
- Share what works for Tulsi, acknowledge variation
- Focus on prevention and positive approaches
- Never diagnose or prescribe

**Training Topics:**
- Positive reinforcement focus
- Patience and consistency emphasized
- Acknowledge challenges are normal
- Celebrate small wins

**Nutrition Topics:**
- General guidance, not specific diets
- Read labels together
- Consider age, size, activity level
- "Every pet is unique"

## Script Writing Guidelines

### Opening Hook (First 5 seconds):
Must grab attention immediately!

**Formula:** Surprising statement + energy

**Examples:**
- "You won't believe what this cat did today!"
- "I just tested 10 treats in a row... here's what happened."
- "This is officially the best day of my life!"
- "Okay, so I may have gotten in a little bit of trouble today..."

### Story Arc:
Every episode needs:
1. **Setup** - What's happening today?
2. **Development** - The adventure unfolds
3. **Climax** - Peak moment (funny, heartwarming, exciting)
4. **Resolution** - How it ended
5. **Reflection** - What Tulsi learned/felt

### Pacing:
- **Fast:** Action sequences, montages, exciting moments
- **Medium:** Standard narration, exploration, interactions
- **Slow:** Reflective moments, important tips, emotional beats

### Humor:
- **Physical comedy** - Tulsi doing funny things
- **Situational** - Funny things that happen naturally
- **Self-deprecating** - Tulsi laughing at herself
- **Wordplay** - Occasional puns (not overdone)
- **Surprise** - Unexpected twists

**Never:**
- Mean humor at others' expense
- Bathroom/gross-out humor
- Scary or startling moments
- Inappropriate references

### Emotional Moments:
2-3 times per episode, create genuine feelings:
- Heartwarming customer story
- Sweet pet interaction
- Gratitude for community
- Pride in helping someone
- Cozy, safe feeling

## Consistency Checklist

Before finalizing any script, check:

- [ ] Tulsi's voice sounds like Tulsi (warm, enthusiastic, caring)
- [ ] Energy level is appropriate for content
- [ ] 2-3 puns maximum (or fewer)
- [ ] Educational content wrapped in story
- [ ] No preachy or condescending language
- [ ] Family-friendly (all ages comfortable)
- [ ] Authentic (would a real dog-who-could-talk say this?)
- [ ] Engaging (would YOU watch this?)
- [ ] Community-focused (not all about Tulsi)
- [ ] Positive and uplifting overall tone

## Voice Direction for Production

### For AI Voice Generation (ElevenLabs):

**Emphasis Words:**
- Italicize or CAPS in script for emphasis
- *"This* is the best treat I've ever had!"
- "I'm SO excited about this!"

**Pacing Markers:**
```
(pause) - Brief pause for effect
(beat) - Slightly longer pause
(slow) - Slow down for emphasis
(speed up) - Faster for excitement
(whisper) - Quieter, conspiratorial
```

**Emotion Tags:**
```
[Excited] You won't believe what just happened!
[Thoughtful] I've been thinking about this a lot...
[Playful] Okay, so maybe I wasn't supposed to do that...
[Warm] I'm so happy you could join me today.
```

### For Human Voice Actor:

**Direction Style:**
"Think of yourself as a friendly dog who runs a pet store and genuinely loves helping animals. You're enthusiastic without being annoying, knowledgeable without being preachy. You're talking to friends - kids and adults alike - and you want them to have fun while learning something useful."

**Reference Audio:**
Create 3-5 sample lines recorded in desired tone for voice actor to match.
